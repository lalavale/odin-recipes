Spaghetti

Description

Spaghetti Bolognese is a hearty Italian dish featuring a rich, savory meat sauce served over perfectly cooked spaghetti. The sauce, made from a blend of ground beef and pork, is simmered with tomatoes, onions, garlic, and a medley of Italian herbs like oregano, basil, and thyme. Carrots and celery add a subtle sweetness and depth to the sauce. A splash of red wine enhances the flavors, and a hint of cream gives it a velvety texture.

This robust sauce is generously ladled over al dente spaghetti and typically garnished with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and a sprinkle of chopped parsley. Each bite is a comforting blend of tangy tomatoes, tender meat, and aromatic herbs, making it a beloved classic.

Ingredients

For sauce:

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, finely diced
  • 1 celery stalk, finely diced
  • 500 grams ground beef and pork
  • 400 grams canned tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For spaghetti:

  • 400 grams spaghetti
  • Salt, for the pasta water

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil** in a large pan. Add finely chopped onion, garlic, carrot, and celery. Saut√© until softened. Add ground beef and pork, cook until browned. Stir in tomatoes, tomato paste, beef broth, oregano, basil, and bay leaf. Season with salt and pepper. Simmer for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Cook spaghetti in salted boiling water until al dente. Drain. Combine spaghetti and sauce, toss to coat. Serve with grated Parmesan and chopped parsley or basil.
